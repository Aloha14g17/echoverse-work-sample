Deliverable 3 — Approval Matrix

Clear boundaries protect the Principal's time and prevent costly mistakes. Below is where I act, where I prepare, and where I never touch.

✓ Do Independently

Operational & admin tasks under defined thresholds.

  • Recurring supplier payments already-approved (cleaner, dog walker)
  • Payments < £100 to known vendors
  • Booking research & shortlisting (dinner, gifts, garages)
  • Document collection, filing, tidying transcripts to Drive
  • Pharmacy re-orders, appointment scheduling
  • Chasing missing docs (insurance photos, plumber invoices)
  • Calendar conflict flagging & childcare gap surfacing
  • Verifying suspicious emails via out-of-band channels

Rationale: reversible, low-cost, well-scoped. Speed matters more than approval.

⏸ Prepare for Approval

Financial commitments, calendar changes, external-facing decisions.

  • Any payment > £100 or any new supplier
  • Insurance renewals, subscription renewals, memberships
  • Sending documents/one-pagers to external parties
  • Rebooking flights / rerouting travel
  • Cancelling or pausing services (gym, subscriptions)
  • Anything with the Principal's name/reputation attached
  • Changes to supplier bank details (fraud vector)
  • Gifts, personal purchases above nominal threshold

Rationale: reversible only at cost; requires Principal's judgement or signature.

✗ Never Touch

Banking, legal, and statutory-filing matters.

  • Moving money between accounts, initiating transfers
  • Filing anything directly with Companies House or HMRC
  • Signing contracts, NDAs, or legal correspondence
  • Accessing personal banking portals or 2FA credentials
  • Making tax decisions, VAT treatment, dividend calls
  • Insurance claim admissions or liability statements
  • Passport / ID applications on Principal's behalf
  • Anything the accountant or solicitor should own

Rationale: irreversible, regulated, or requires personal identity. My role is to prepare & hand off.

Deliverable 4 — Five Decisions Requiring Principal Input

Each surfaced with a recommendation and short justification so the Principal can approve in one line.

1. Accept the 50% Council PCN discount extension Recommend: PAY £65 NOW

The original £65 discount window lapsed but the council will honour it if I call before Tuesday; otherwise £130 becomes payable, escalating to £195 in 11 days. Payment is small, reversible only through appeal (which we would lose), and delay costs 100–200% of the original amount.

2. Home insurer's leak claim — complete or decline? Recommend: DECLINE PROCESSING

The claim value is marginal and we are missing two photos plus the plumber's invoice, which the plumber has not responded to in three weeks. Continuing risks the insurer disputing the whole claim on incomplete evidence and could affect the next renewal premium. Better to let it close cleanly and absorb the cost.

3. Gym membership renewal (£499) Recommend: RENEW UNDER PRINCIPAL'S NAME, 1 MONTH TRIAL

The Principal's own note ("do I even use this??") signals genuine ambivalence. Usage data over the last 3 months averages twice a month — below break-even vs. pay-as-you-go. I recommend renewing month-to-month under the Principal's personal name (not Echoverse), setting a 30-day usage review; cancel if unchanged.

4. Echoverse Collective Ltd — Companies House confirmation statement Recommend: ACCOUNTANT FILES, NOT EA

Statutory filings sit firmly in the "never touch" zone. I will prepare the confirmation-statement pack (register of members, PSC data, SIC codes) and hand it to Meridian Partners by end of week so they can file within the 12-day window. Principal only needs to confirm no changes to shareholders/PSC.

5. Airline schedule change — new arrival 22:40 Recommend: ACCEPT + BOOK AIRPORT HOTEL

Rebooking to an earlier flight adds £340+ and there is no direct option before the workshop. Accepting the new time and pre-booking a 5-minute-transfer airport hotel gives the Principal ~8 hours of rest before the 09:00 workshop — cheaper, lower-stress, and removes morning-commute risk. Add a 07:30 wake-up call and pre-order breakfast.

Deliverable 5 — SOP: Handling Supplier Invoices

Standard Operating Procedure for all inbound invoices addressed to the Principal or Echoverse entities.

Process Owner: Executive Assistant (EA)
Applies To: All personal + Echoverse-entity supplier invoices

Scope: Any request for payment received via email, WhatsApp, physical post (photo), or supplier portal — including recurring, ad-hoc, and disputed invoices. Excludes payroll and HMRC obligations.

Procedure

1. Intake. Log invoice in Monday.com "Supplier Invoices" board within 4 working hours. Capture: supplier name, amount, currency, invoice #, date received, entity (personal / Echoverse Ltd / Echoverse Collective), due date, PO reference if any. Attach original file to record.
2. Triage. Classify into: (a) recurring, previously approved (cleaner, dog walker, hosting) → route to Queue; (b) new supplier or amount changed → route to Approval; (c) suspected fraud / bank-details changed / duplicate → route to Escalation. Cross-check every invoice against paid-remittance file (catches duplicates like Halcyon £480).
3. Queue. Group approved invoices into a twice-weekly payment run (Tue & Fri, 14:00). Verify amounts, IBAN/sort-code against stored supplier record, and reference field. Never accept a bank-detail change by email alone — always confirm by phone using a number already on file.
4. Approval. For invoices > £100 or new suppliers, prepare a one-line summary + attached invoice + recommendation, present via the WhatsApp Update or Notion Approval Queue. Principal responds ✅ / ❌ / hold. Log timestamped decision.
5. Payment. Principal (or accountant, for Echoverse) executes the actual bank payment — EA never touches transfers. EA confirms the payment reference against the supplier record once remittance is generated.
6. Follow-up. Email/WhatsApp the supplier a remittance advice within 24 hours of payment. Update Monday.com status to "Paid" with date + reference. For chased invoices, note it in a "Reliability" field so we can trigger earlier payment next cycle.
7. Documentation. File PDF invoice + remittance in Google Drive: /Finance/[Entity]/[YYYY]/[MM]/[Supplier]_[Invoice#].pdf. Tag Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 so accountant can pull the quarter in one query.

Human Checkpoints

  • Any bank-detail change → verbal confirmation with supplier
  • Any invoice > £100 or new supplier → Principal approval
  • Any Echoverse-entity invoice → Principal approval regardless of amount
  • Any suspected fraud/phishing → escalate to Principal + accountant same day

Tools Used

  • Monday.com — Supplier Invoices board (source of truth)
  • Google Drive — canonical PDF storage
  • Notion — Approval Queue for pending Principal decisions
  • WhatsApp Business — Principal-facing summaries + supplier remittances
  • Xero / QuickBooks — accountant handoff for Echoverse entities

Escalation Triggers

  • Third chase received on an invoice EA has previously logged
  • Any change of supplier bank details (immediate freeze)
  • Invoice mismatched to remittance (duplicate or overpayment)
  • Supplier threatens legal action or credit-agency referral
  • Invoice from an unknown entity claiming prior relationship

Deliverable 6 — AI Workflow Proposal

"Weekly Principal Triage & Decision Brief" — an automated pipeline that ingests inbound signals, drafts a triaged brief, and delivers it as the Monday 07:00 WhatsApp update.

Tools & Stack

  • Make.com (primary orchestrator) — polls Gmail, Google Drive, and Notion every 15 minutes
  • Claude 3.5 Sonnet / GPT-4 — classification, summarisation, draft-decision generation
  • Notion API — persistent state for the Approval Queue and Task Tracker mirror
  • Monday.com API — writes triaged items back to the execution board
  • WhatsApp Business API (Twilio) — delivers the Monday 07:00 brief and captures Principal replies
  • Plaud + Whisper — voice-note transcription

Workflow Steps

  1. Ingest — Make.com watches: Gmail (inbox + filters), a designated WhatsApp forwarding number, Drive uploads (Plaud transcripts), and photo uploads to a shared album.
  2. Classify — LLM tags each item with: urgency, category (finance / calendar / suppliers / family / Echoverse), suggested approval bucket (Do / Prep / Never), and confidence score.
  3. Deduplicate & enrich — cross-reference against remittance file & last-30-days task log to catch duplicates like the Halcyon £480 invoice.
  4. Draft brief — every Sunday 20:00, the LLM composes: (a) URGENT items, (b) items needing approval with a recommended answer, (c) FYI/scheduled. Human checkpoint 1: EA reviews and edits the draft.
  5. Send — Monday 07:00 WhatsApp Business delivers the ≤200-word update to the Principal.
  6. Capture decisions — Principal replies with ✅/❌/hold + optional note; parser writes decisions back to Monday.com and Notion Approval Queue.
  7. Execute — EA runs the approved actions during the week. Human checkpoint 2: EA never lets AI trigger a payment, filing, or external message directly.
  8. Weekly retro — Friday 17:00 automated summary of what shipped, what slipped, and what should be a new SOP.

Data Governance

May enter AI tools

  • Subject lines, sender domains, invoice amounts, due dates
  • Anonymised task descriptions and calendar entries
  • Public correspondence (newsletters, generic notices)

May NOT enter AI tools

  • Full bank details (account numbers, sort codes, IBANs)
  • National Insurance / passport / tax reference numbers
  • Full email bodies containing legal correspondence or NDAs
  • Medical / pharmacy details beyond "collection needed"
  • Children's names, school details, home address in prompts
  • Any content the Principal has flagged confidential

All prompts are logged; PII is redacted before sending to the model. Zero-retention API endpoints are used where available; no training on data.

Expected Time Savings

Manual triage today: ~6–8 hrs/week across inbox, WhatsApp, voice notes.
With workflow: ~1.5–2 hrs/week for EA review + execution — ≈70% saving.

The Principal reclaims Monday morning: instead of a 45-minute catch-up call, they get a 90-second WhatsApp read with one-tap decisions.
